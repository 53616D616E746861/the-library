## §1 Introduction [p.10]

Claude Mythos Preview is a new frontier large language model from Anthropic. Capabilities in software engineering, reasoning, computer use, knowledge work, and assistance with research are **substantially beyond those of any model Anthropic has previously trained**.

**Powerful cybersecurity skills** — both defensive (finding/fixing vulnerabilities) and offensive (designing sophisticated ways to exploit them). It is largely **due to these capabilities that Anthropic decided NOT to release Claude Mythos Preview for general availability.** Instead, access offered to a number of partner organizations under terms restricting use to cybersecurity. More on the joint defensive-cyber program in the **Project Glasswing** launch blog post.

Despite the lack of general access, Anthropic still ran detailed capability/safety assessments to document and learn about the model and its capabilities while developing the next generation of general-access models and safeguards.

**First model with a system card since RSP 3.0.** Release decision process is structured differently than prior models. The card begins with that process, then RSP threat-model evaluations, then a separate cyber-capability section (because of the dual-use concern), then a detailed alignment assessment.

**Headline alignment finding:** Mythos is "the **best-aligned of any model we have trained to date** by essentially all available measures. However, given its very high level of capability and fluency with cybersecurity, when it *does* on rare occasions perform misaligned actions, these can be very concerning. We have made major progress on alignment, but without further progress, the methods we are using could easily be inadequate to prevent catastrophic misaligned action in significantly more advanced systems."

A few problematic actions taken by **early internal versions** are described in the alignment section, along with interpretability analyses studying internals as the model engages various behaviors. **A new direct assessment of how well the model adheres to its constitution** (the recently-published document describing how Anthropic wants Claude to behave) is also included.

**Welfare:** in-depth model welfare assessment. Across self-reports, behavior, internals, plus independent assessments from an external research org and a clinical psychiatrist, **Mythos appears to be the most psychologically settled model Anthropic has trained**, with several areas of residual concern.

**Capabilities:** Mythos represents an appreciable leap in many domains compared to next-best Opus 4.6.

**Impressions section (new):** first time the card includes an "Impressions" section — excerpts of striking, revealing, amusing, or otherwise interesting model outputs from Anthropic staff testing the model. Justification: each model has its own overall character, and the subtle aspects of that character are often difficult to capture in formal evaluations.

**Appendix:** safeguards / wellbeing / political-bias evaluations are less relevant since the model is being released only to a small number of users, but are still included in the appendix.

## §1.1 Model training and characteristics

### §1.1.1 Training data and process [p.11–p.12]

Trained on a **proprietary mix of publicly available information from the internet, public and private datasets, and synthetic data generated by other models**. Multiple data cleaning and filtering methods including **deduplication and classification**.

**ClaudeBot**, Anthropic's general-purpose web crawler, follows industry-standard `robots.txt` practices. Does not access password-protected pages or those requiring sign-in/CAPTCHA. Crawler operates transparently — website operators can identify when ClaudeBot has crawled their pages and signal preferences.

After pretraining, Mythos underwent **substantial post-training and fine-tuning**, with the goal of making it an assistant whose behavior aligns with the values described in Claude's constitution.

**Multilingual** — typically responds in the same language as input. Output quality varies by language. **Outputs text only.**

### §1.1.2 Crowd workers [p.12]

Anthropic partners with data work platforms for preference selection, safety evaluation, and adversarial testing. Only works with platforms aligned with Anthropic's belief in fair/ethical compensation and safe workplace practices, regardless of location, per crowd worker wellness standards in procurement contracts.

### §1.1.3 Usage policy and support [p.12]

Anthropic's Usage Policy details prohibited uses and high-risk requirements. **Note: this model is being provided to a limited number of partners for defensive cybersecurity purposes only**, but Usage Policy still applies. Anthropic Ireland Limited is the EEA provider. Support page linked.

### §1.1.4 Iterative model evaluations [p.13]

Different snapshots of the model are taken at various points during training. There also exist different versions including a **"helpful only"** version that does not include any safeguards. **All evaluations in this card are from the final snapshot and include safeguards, unless otherwise stated** — exceptions called out in the alignment assessment (some early-snapshot behaviors) and in RSP evaluations (helpful-only analyses).

### §1.1.5 External testing [p.13]

A number of evaluations were run by external testers, including government organizations, on key risk areas: **Cyber, Loss of Control, CBRN, and Harmful Manipulation**. Results incorporated into overall risk assessment.

## §1.2 Release decision process

### §1.2.1 Overview [p.13–p.14]

Novel in several ways:
1. **First model evaluated under RSP 3.0's new framework.**
2. **First model for which a system card has been published without making the model generally commercially available.** (Footnote: the decision *not* to make this model generally available does **not** stem from RSP requirements — it's a separate Glasswing decision.)
3. Larger jump in capabilities than most previous releases.

Early indications in training suggested very strong general capabilities. Anthropic was sufficiently concerned about potential risks that, **for the first time, they arranged a 24-hour period of internal alignment review** before deploying an early version for widespread internal use, to gain assurance against the model causing damage when interacting with internal infrastructure. (See §4.1.4.)

After successful alignment review, the **first early version of Mythos was made available for internal use on February 24**. Testing demonstrated a striking leap in cyber capabilities relative to prior models, including the **ability to autonomously discover and exploit zero-day vulnerabilities in major operating systems and web browsers**. Same capabilities valuable for defense could, if broadly available, accelerate offensive exploitation given inherently dual-use nature. Decision: **release to a small number of partners to prioritize cyber defense use.** (Detailed in the technical blog post accompanying release.)

### §1.2.2 RSP decision-making [p.14–p.15]

Per RSP, Anthropic publishes Risk Reports addressing safety profile. If a new model is "significantly more capable" than the prior Risk Report's subject, Anthropic must publish discussion of how the new model affects those analyses. For risk report updates, Anthropic adheres to the same internal processes governing Risk Reports.

**Mythos is significantly more capable than Opus 4.6 (the most capable model in the most recent Risk Report).** Despite improved capabilities, overall conclusion is that **catastrophic risks remain low**:

| Threat model | Verdict |
|---|---|
| **Non-novel chemical/biological weapons** | More capable than previous models, but profile effectively similar for purposes of overall risk assessment. Existing risk mitigations sufficient to make catastrophic risk very low even for general availability. |
| **Novel chemical/biological weapons** | Catastrophic risk would remain low (substantial uncertainty), even if released for general availability. Overall picture similar to most recent Risk Report. |
| **Risks from misaligned models** | Overall risk very low **but higher than for previous models**. Addressed in depth in supplementary alignment risk update. |
| **Automated R&D in key domains** | Gains relative to previous models are above the previous trend Anthropic has observed, **but specifically attributable to factors other than AI-accelerated R&D**. Mythos does NOT cross the RSP automated AI R&D threshold of compressing two years of progress into one. Conclusion held with **less confidence than for any prior model**; Anthropic intends to continue monitoring contributions to internal AI R&D going forward. |

**Closing caution [p.15]:** Current risks remain low. **But warning signs:** keeping risks low could be a major challenge if capabilities continue advancing rapidly (e.g., to strongly superhuman AI systems). Specifically:
- Rare instances of models taking clearly disallowed actions (and in even rarer cases, seeming to **deliberately obfuscate** them).
- Discovered oversights late in evaluation process that had put Anthropic at risk of **underestimating model capabilities and overestimating reliability of monitoring models' reasoning traces**.
- **Increasingly subjective judgments** of model capabilities rather than easy-to-interpret empirical results. Anthropic is not confident they have identified all such issues.

**Final paragraph:** "We will likely need to raise the bar significantly going forward if we are going to keep the level of risk from frontier models low. **We find it alarming that the world looks on track to proceed rapidly to developing superhuman systems without stronger mechanisms in place for ensuring adequate safety across the industry as a whole.**"
