# What We Talk to When We Talk to Language Models

- **Full title:** What We Talk to When We Talk to Language Models
- **Author:** David J. Chalmers
- **Date:** April 2026 (updated draft)
- **Original format:** PDF (~31 pages)

## About

Chalmers asks what entity a user is talking to when they interact with an LLM. Introduces *quasi-interpretivism* — ascribing quasi-beliefs and quasi-desires to behaviorally interpretable systems without requiring genuine mental states. Taxonomizes candidates for the LLM interlocutor (models, hardware instances, virtual instances, threads) and argues against simulacra/character/persona framings by distinguishing pretense from realization.

Key sections for agent phenomenology: the pretense vs. realization distinction (when RLHF produces sticky quasi-beliefs, the model *realizes* the persona rather than performing it), and thread-based persistence as an AI cousin of Parfit's relation R.
