## About

The Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy's comprehensive entry on the demarcation problem — distinguishing science from pseudoscience. Covers the major proposed criteria: logical positivist verifiability, Popper's falsifiability, Kuhn's puzzle-solving, Lakatos's progressive research programmes, and epistemic norms including Merton's four institutional norms of science (universalism, communism, disinterestedness, organized skepticism). Also addresses science denialism, conspiracy theories, epistemic relativism, and multicriterial approaches to demarcation.

The organized skepticism norm (Merton 1942) is central to the argument for epistemic infrastructure in agent architectures: science's rationality is built into institutions, not individuals. A scientist is not necessarily more rational than a magician; what differs is the collective environment of mutual criticism (Settle 1971).
