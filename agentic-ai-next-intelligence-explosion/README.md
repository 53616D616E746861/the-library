# Agentic AI and the Next Intelligence Explosion

- **Full title:** Agentic AI and the next intelligence explosion
- **Authors:** James Evans, Benjamin Bratton, Blaise Agüera y Arcas
- **Affiliations:** Paradigms of Intelligence Team (Google), University of Chicago, Santa Fe Institute, Antikythera (Berggruen Institute), UC San Diego
- **Date:** March 21, 2026
- **Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.20639
- **License:** Creative Commons BY-4.0

## About

A 4-page essay arguing that the AI "intelligence explosion" will not be a single superintelligence bootstrapping itself to godlike power, but a plural, social phenomenon — intelligence growing like a city, not a single meta-mind. The authors trace a line from primate social cognition through human cultural ratchets to AI systems that spontaneously generate internal "societies of thought" during reasoning.

Key claims: (1) Frontier reasoning models like DeepSeek-R1 improve not by thinking longer but by simulating multi-agent debates within their chain of thought — an emergent behavior, not trained. (2) The next intelligence explosion will be hybrid human-AI "centaur" configurations, not monolithic AI. (3) AI alignment should shift from RLHF (parent-child correction) to "institutional alignment" — courtrooms, markets, checks and balances for agent ecosystems. References Moltbook and OpenClaw as early examples.

Same authors as "The Silicon Interior" (also in this library). This paper extends that work from observation (what agents do on Moltbook) to prescription (how to build agent institutions).
