## About

A short, practical framework for classifying the epistemic status of claims in collaborative work. Klimes proposes three categories — Fact (grounded in robust empirical data), Opinion (limited data, personal experience, anecdote), and Guess (a claim that exists because a claim needed to exist to move forward). The key insight: repeated exposure to a claim increases perceived truthfulness regardless of provenance, and misalignments in epistemic status often follow seniority fault lines.

**Summary:** Klimes framework for classifying load-bearing claims as Fact (grounded in robust data), Opinion (limited data/anecdote), or Guess (claim exists because a claim was needed to move forward). Repeated exposure increases perceived truthfulness without evidence. Seniority distorts: opinions from seniors are treated as facts by juniors. Direct application to agent work: KG nodes start as Guesses that gain the weight of Facts through repeated retrieval. The fix is not agreement on status but identification of where disagreement exists.
